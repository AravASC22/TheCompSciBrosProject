Introduction

Contrary to popular belief, computer science is not solely the practice of coding. Computer Science is the study of automation, computation, information, algorithmic thinking, and mathematics. Having a strong foundation in computer science can lead to a much easier journey learning how to program! Even if programming is not your goal, there are various enticing opportunities within the computer science industry!

According to the U.S Bureau of Labor and Statistics, careers in the field of copmuter science are projected to grow 22 percent from 2020 to 2030. Additionally, careers in computer science can be very lucrative with an average salary between $62,000 and $160,000!

Basic knowledge of coding and the thought process involved when coding are incredibly helpful to know when pursuing a career in the computer science field. While there are many career opportunities here that do not require programming skills. Having basic knowledge of them even if you aren't good at it or plan to pursue it is still helpful.

W3Schools

This website contains information on how to use many different functions and gives a demonstration of them in action that you can edit. While this website isn't the best for starting out or learning how to complete specific tasks, it allows you to figure out how the parts you'll need to complete a larger task actually work.

Visit W3Schools!

Stack Overflow

This website is a public forum where people ask questions about code. More often than not, somebody has already asked the question you seek an answer to. This can be very helpful for solving specific problems you encounter while completing a task.

Visit Stack Overflow!

Project Euler

This website is a collection of coding challenges that are great for learning how to code. If there's anything you don't know how to do, you can refer to the other two sites! If all else fails, Google is your friend. It may feel discouraging to not know what to do and google commands, but that's part of the process. Rome wasn't built in a day!

Coding Bootcamps

Enrolling in a coding bootcamp is another up and coming way to transition into the field of computer science and technolgogy. Coding bootcamps are accelerated programs where subjects such as Full-Stack Web Development, Data Science, Digital Marketing, and UX/UI Design are taught. Many people who attend these bootcamps may have had a technical background in other subjects such as math, physics, biology, chemistry, etc, or are transitioning from one field into the tech industry. Coding bootcamps usually have an intensive curriculum that can consist of website development programming languages like JavaScript, Ruby on Rails, PHP, and other languages such as Java and C#. Coding bootcamps can range from part-time for people in a career to full time. Additionally, these bootcamps can range anywhere fro $1,300 t0 $30,000.

Careers and Salaries

Many people who study computer science go into the software engineering role. Although this role is the most common, there are many other roles within the tech industry. Here are a few:

Product Manager:

A product manager is the person who identifies the customer need and the larger business objectives that a product or feature will fulfill, articulates what success looks like for a product, and rallies a team to turn that vision into a reality.

Average Salary: $61-150k

UX/UI design:

A UI/UX designer's job is to create user-friendly interfaces that enable users to understand how to use complex technical products. If you're passionate about the latest technology trends and devices, you'll find great fulfillment in being involved in the design process for the next hot gadget. Switching between tasks.

Average Salary: $51-120k.

Data Scientist:

Data scientists help organizations to solve vexing problems. Combining computer science, modeling, statistics, analytics, and math skills—along with sound business sense—data scientists uncover the answers to major questions that help organizations make objective decisions.

Average Salary: $67-150k.

Software Engineer:

Software engineering is the branch of computer science that deals with the design, development, testing, and maintenance of software applications. Software engineers apply engineering principles and knowledge of programming languages to build software solutions for end users.

Average Salary: $66-140k.
